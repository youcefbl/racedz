<Callout type="tip" title="First things first: your shoes">
Before any accessory, the most important — and most valuable — thing you can spend money on is a **good pair of running shoes**. The right shoes mean comfortable runs and, above all, fewer injuries — nothing on this list matters if your feet hurt. Get those right first (see our [best running shoes guide](/blog/best-running-shoes-algeria)), then the accessories below make everything better.
</Callout>

Once you have shoes and a way to track your runs, a few small accessories make running in Algeria dramatically more comfortable — and safer. Our climate is the deciding factor: long hot summers, strong sun, and dry air mean the gear that matters here isn't the same as in a cool European city. Here's what earns its place.

## 1. Real running socks

The cheapest upgrade with the biggest payoff. Cotton socks hold sweat and cause blisters; technical running socks wick moisture and have zero seams where they rub.

Buy two or three pairs. Anything labelled "running" from Kalenji/Decathlon or a known brand beats cotton instantly. Your feet will thank you on the first long run.
</ProductPick>

## 2. Hydration you can carry

In Algerian heat, waiting until you're thirsty is too late. For anything over 45 minutes, or any summer run, carry water.

- **Handheld bottle** — simplest, cheapest, great for 5–15 km.
- **Hydration belt** — 1–2 small bottles around your waist, hands free.
- **Hydration vest** — for long runs and trail; carries 1.5–2 L plus phone and keys.

<Callout type="warning" title="Heat is not a small thing">
Heatstroke is a real risk in an Algerian summer. Run at **dawn or after sunset**, carry water, and if you feel dizzy, stop walking and get into shade. No session is worth it.
</Callout>

## 3. Cap and sunglasses

A light-coloured running cap keeps the sun off your face and holds sweat back; sunglasses cut glare so you're not squinting for an hour. Both are small, cheap, and make midday light bearable.

## 4. Anti-chafe balm

On longer runs, skin rubbing against skin or fabric turns raw — inner thighs, underarms, and (for men) nipples. A stick of anti-chafe balm prevents all of it.

Body Glide is the dedicated product, but a cheap tub of Vaseline from any pharmacy does the same job. Apply before you head out.
</ProductPick>

## 5. Technical clothing — skip the cotton

A cotton t-shirt in July becomes a heavy, sweat-soaked sponge. A cheap technical (polyester) running shirt weighs nothing wet, dries fast, and cuts chafing. This one swap makes summer running far more pleasant.

## 6. A light and reflective gear — for dawn and dusk runs

Because the smart move in summer is running when it's cool, you'll often be out in low light. Be seen:

- A **head torch** so you can see the road at dawn.
- A **reflective band or bright top** so cars see you.
