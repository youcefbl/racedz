The single piece of gear that most changes how running *feels* is the shoe on your foot. The right pair protects your knees, keeps blisters away, and makes the difference between dreading a run and looking forward to it. The tricky part in Algeria isn't knowing which shoes are good — it's finding pairs that are actually available here without paying import prices that make no sense.

This guide focuses on shoes you can realistically get: models sold in Decathlon Algérie, imported through local sports shops, or ordered online and delivered to Algerian addresses. We've grouped them by what you actually need.

<Callout type="tip" title="How to pick, in one line">
For your first pair, prioritise a **cushioned daily trainer in your correct size** over any specific brand or hype model. Comfort and fit beat marketing every time.
</Callout>

## How to buy the right size

- **Measure late in the day**, when your feet are at their largest.
- Leave about **a thumb's width** of space in front of your longest toe. Running shoes should be roughly half a size bigger than your everyday shoes.
- Your heel should lock in place with no slipping; the toe box should let your toes wiggle.
- If you can, **try before a run, not after sitting all day** — feet swell when you run.
